## Why Georgia (Not Delaware)

| | **Georgia** | **Delaware** |
|---|---|---|
| **Filing fee** | $110 | $110 |
| **Annual fee** | $60/yr | $300/yr |
| **Privacy** | Member names public | Members NOT public |
| **State income tax** | 5.49% (you pay this anyway as GA resident) | None on out-of-state income |
| **Registered agent** | Must have GA address | Must have DE address |
| **Series LLC** | Not available | Available |

**Bottom line:** Forming in Delaware while living/operating in Georgia means paying BOTH states (DE $300/yr + GA foreign registration $225 + $50/yr). Georgia only = $110 + $60/yr. Delaware's advantages (Court of Chancery, privacy, Series LLC) don't matter at this stage.

## Step-by-Step Registration (Online)

### Step 1: Choose Your LLC Name
- Search availability: [GA Business Search](https://ecorp.sos.ga.gov/BusinessSearch)
- Must include "LLC" or "L.L.C."
- Can't match an existing Georgia business
- Can enter 2nd and 3rd choice names as backups

**Holding company naming conventions:** "[Name] Holdings LLC", "[Name] Capital LLC", "[Name] Group LLC"

### Step 2: Choose a Registered Agent

**Option A — Be your own ($0):**
- Your address goes on public record
- Must be a GA physical address (no PO boxes)
- Must be available during business hours for legal mail

**Option B — Hire a service (~$39-125/yr):**
- Keeps personal address off public records
- They scan and forward mail
- Recommended: Northwest Registered Agent (~$39/yr first year)

**Recommendation:** Registered agent service is worth it for a holding company. More professional, keeps apartment address private.

### Step 3: File Articles of Organization Online

1. Go to [GA Corporations Division](https://ecorp.sos.ga.gov/Account)
2. Create a user account
3. Click "Create or Register a Business"
4. Select "Domestic Limited Liability Company"
5. Fill in:
   - **LLC Name** — with "LLC" designator
   - **Business Purpose** — Select "Any legal purpose" (keeps it flexible)
   - **Principal Office Address** — Your GA address
   - **Business Email** — Personal email is fine
   - **Registered Agent** — Create yourself or search for hired service
   - **Organizer Info** — You (the person submitting)
   - **Optional Provisions** — Leave blank
6. Pay $110 (credit card)
7. **Approval: 7-10 business days**

You'll receive via email:
- Approved Articles of Organization
- Certificate of Organization (needed for bank account)

### Step 4: Draft an Operating Agreement

Internal document — NOT filed with the state. Keep with business records.

**Required by banks** to open a business account. Critical for:
- Proving LLC legitimacy in court
- Defining ownership and profit distribution
- Establishing management structure

**For a single-member LLC, it covers:**
- Sole member and manager designation
- Profit/loss allocation (100% to you)
- Capital contributions
- Procedure for adding members later
- Dissolution procedures

**Status:** Atlas will draft a tailored operating agreement.

### Step 5: Get an EIN

1. Go to [IRS EIN Online Application](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online)
2. Select "Limited Liability Company"
3. Enter your info + LLC details
4. **Done in 15 minutes** — EIN issued immediately

### Step 6: Open a Business Bank Account

**Bring to the bank:**
- Certificate of Organization (from Step 3)
- Operating Agreement (from Step 4)
- EIN confirmation letter (from Step 5)
- Personal ID

**Critical rule:** Keep business and personal finances COMPLETELY separate. This maintains liability protection.

## Tax Implications

### Default: Disregarded Entity (Single-Member LLC)
- LLC doesn't file its own federal return
- Profits/losses flow to personal 1040 via Schedule C
- **Self-employment tax:** 15.3% on LLC net income (Social Security + Medicare)
- **Georgia state income tax:** 5.49%
- **No separate LLC tax** — it all passes through to you

### Future Optimization: S-Corp Election
- Once LLC generates **$70k+ net income consistently**
- File Form 2553 with IRS
- Pay yourself a "reasonable salary" → subject to payroll tax
- Take remaining profits as distributions → NOT subject to self-employment tax
- **Savings:** Can be significant (thousands/year) once income is high enough

### Quarterly Estimated Taxes
- Unlike W-2 income, LLC income has no employer withholding
- Must pay quarterly estimated taxes (April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15)
- Underpayment penalty if you owe >$1,000 at filing

### Important Timing
- Form LLC in 2026 → first Annual Registration due **April 1, 2027**
- **Pro tip:** If forming in Oct-Dec and don't need it active yet, forward-date to January to avoid an extra year of annual registration

## Delaware: When It Would Make Sense

Revisit Delaware if/when:
- Raising outside investors who expect Delaware governance
- Building something that might get acquired (acquirers prefer Delaware)
- Need Series LLC structure for compartmentalized investments (real estate + stocks + PE as separate series)
- Privacy becomes critical
- Revenue justifies the extra $290/yr in fees

---

## Why Delaware Is Popular (For Reference)

1. **Court of Chancery** — Dedicated business court, no juries, judges who specialize in corporate law. Faster, more predictable dispute resolution.
2. **100+ years of case law** — Most predictable legal outcomes in the country for business disputes.
3. **Privacy** — Member/manager names not required on formation docs.
4. **No state income tax** on out-of-state revenue.
5. **Maximum flexibility** in operating agreements — voting rights, profit distribution, management structure.
6. **Series LLC** — One LLC with multiple segregated series, each with its own assets/liabilities.
